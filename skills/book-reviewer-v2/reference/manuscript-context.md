# Manuscript Context: The Road Beneath Dragon Wings

This reference supports the `book-reviewer` skill.

The manuscript is a fantasy novelization of a D&D campaign, but the target experience is a coherent fantasy novel, not a campaign log.

## Core Identity

Working title / project umbrella:

**The Road Beneath Dragon Wings**

Central figure:

**Gilbert de Vere / Gilbert Veringard**

Gilbert's identity, inheritance, divine favor, personal agency, and relationship to the Veringard name are major long-term concerns.

## Major Concerns to Track

- Gilbert's divided identity as de Vere/Veringard
- Bahamut's favor, silence, and mystery
- Whether Gilbert's power comes from divine favor, inheritance, instinct, or personal agency
- The Shield of the Hidden Lord as both necessary and dangerous
- Charlie's relationship with Hazirawn and infernal temptation
- Oolong's grimoire and forbidden-knowledge thread
- Skwerker's prophecy and transformation
- Dossi's prophecy, especially the "forsaken soldier" thread
- Vollum, Maccath, proof, infection, and what the party carries home
- Council politics and consequences
- Travel, ships, portals, and chapter-boundary geography
- D&D mechanics becoming fiction rather than visible mechanics
- Combat as character and consequence, not only action

## Reader Experience Goals

The reader should understand:

- Who wants what
- Why each journey matters
- What changed because of each chapter
- Why victories cost something
- Why repeated motifs matter
- Why the party's actions reshape politics, faith, and danger

The reader should not need to know D&D rules to follow the story.

## Audiobook Goals

The listener should be able to follow:

- Scene transitions
- Speaker identity
- Combat geography
- Proper nouns
- Emotional turns
- Prophecy and callback language
- Which repeated words are motifs and which are accidental repetition
